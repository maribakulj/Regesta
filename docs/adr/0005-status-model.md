# 0005 — Dual status model: machine truth and human workflow

- Status: Accepted
- Date: 2026-04-17

## Context

Every assertion carries a `status`. That field answers the question: "how
should the pipeline and its users treat this assertion?" Two distinct
questions hide behind that one word:

1. **Machine truth.** Is this assertion currently in force? Has it been
   superseded by a later pass? Has it been retracted? Is it a proposal that
   the pipeline itself has not confirmed?
2. **Human workflow.** Has a human reviewer accepted or rejected this
   assertion? Does it require review before being projected?

Collapsing these into a single axis either conflates them (losing
information) or over-specializes toward one use case.

The provenance field already records *where* an assertion came from (source
/ pass / rule). Duplicating that as status values (`:normalized`, `:inferred`)
is redundant. What is not captured elsewhere is the **epistemic** status
(machine) and the **workflow** status (human).

## Decision

`status` is a single enum with two disjoint families:

**Machine truth:**
- `:asserted` — in force, produced by ingestion or by an executed rule
- `:proposed` — generated by a repair or inference rule, not auto-accepted
- `:retracted` — deliberately withdrawn (e.g. by a repair that replaces it)
- `:superseded` — replaced by a more recent assertion of higher confidence

**Human workflow:**
- `:accepted` — a human reviewer endorsed a proposal
- `:rejected` — a human reviewer rejected a proposal
- `:needs-review` — flagged for mandatory human inspection

An assertion's status belongs to exactly one family at a time. A `:proposed`
assertion becomes `:accepted` (or `:rejected`) when a reviewer acts;
accepting a proposal may cause the original to become `:superseded`.

Origin information (source, pass, rule) lives in `provenance`, not in
`status`.

## Alternatives considered

- **Single-axis status mixing truth and provenance.** Rejected: `:normalized`
  and `:inferred` are properties of the producing pass, already captured by
  provenance. Repeating them in `status` is a source of drift.
- **Separate `:truth-status` and `:workflow-status` fields.** Rejected for
  V1: adds a field that is almost always meaningful in only one family at a
  time. A unified enum with clear family boundaries is simpler.
- **Boolean `accepted?` flag alongside a machine status.** Rejected:
  insufficient granularity (no way to express `:needs-review`).

## Consequences

- Machine passes operate on `:asserted` and `:proposed` assertions; they
  ignore `:retracted` and `:superseded` by default.
- Projection considers only `:asserted` and `:accepted` assertions unless a
  pipeline explicitly opts in to proposals.
- The `apply-repairs` workflow has clear inputs (`:proposed`) and outputs
  (`:accepted`, `:rejected`, `:needs-review`), making human review a
  first-class operation.
- Status transitions are small and auditable; the provenance trail records
  every transition with its rule and timestamp.
